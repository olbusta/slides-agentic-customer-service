# RCMP Agentic Ideas

**Author:** Olof Bustamante

---

## Idea 1 — Pre-delivery exception
A stock issue is flagged before Sarah notices. The RCMP agent reaches out, explains the delay, and offers an alternative that ships on time. She never has to call. Fewer inbound contacts, fewer cancellations, higher NPS.

> Strong business value — directly reduces inbound contact volume and protects conversion.

---

## Idea 2 — Assembly escalation
Sarah has been stuck on the same AR assembly step for 8+ minutes. The agent steps in: "This one trips a lot of people up — want me to connect you with someone who can walk you through it live?" One tap, live support. Shows the full assistive → proactive → human arc in a single moment.

> Operationally complex — requires live agent availability and real-time AR behaviour tracking.

---

## Idea 3 — The 6-month proactive replace
The agent has flagged a known seam issue on Sarah's sofa cover model. Before she experiences it: "A small number of customers with your cover have reported an issue. We've looked at your purchase — if you're seeing this, we'll replace it. If not, just ignore this." Resolves a complaint before it exists. The most trust-building moment in the journey.

> High emotional impact — best for the vision narrative, even if longer term to implement.

---

## Idea 5 — The missing item catch
Sarah finishes her store visit and orders that evening. The agent cross-references her room design against her basket — the rug she planned around isn't in the order. "Looks like your rug isn't in your basket — want to add it?" A genuine catch, not a push. Incremental revenue with real customer value behind it.

> Quick win — relatively simple to implement, clear revenue upside, feels helpful not pushy.
